# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Creative technologist, writer, and builder — the site's owner and primary curator. Secondary audience: potential collaborators, design-forward employers, and fellow writers who discover the site through shared essays or project links.

Context: browsing on desktop during working hours, on tablet or phone during idle moments. The reader comes for a single piece (essay or project) and stays for the atmosphere — the site itself is a credential.

## Product Purpose

A personal website that presents two interleaved bodies of work: personal essays (随笔/生活记录) and project showcases (项目展示). Essays are the primary content; project pages are the secondary axis.

Success looks like: a visitor lands on an essay, reads it through without distraction, then naturally browses to the project showcase or another essay because the typographic rhythm and spatial clarity made the stay effortless. The site itself — its Bauhaus-informed restraint, sharp contrast, and deliberate emptiness — is a proof of craft.

## Brand Personality

Minimalist. Sharp. Restrained. (极简·锋利·克制)

Influenced by the Bauhaus tradition: geometric clarity, structural honesty, strong contrast without ornament. The tone is warm enough for personal essays but disciplined enough for work presentation. No sentimentality, no decorative excess.

## Anti-references

- **Boring / monotonous.** Generic landing page templates, identical card grids, the hero-metric template, stock illustrations. Any layout pattern that could be guessed from a category alone.
- **SaaS-cream minimalism.** Soft shadows, rounded everything, gradient buttons, "friendly startup" visual language.
- **Noise masquerading as personality.** Overengineered scroll animations, particle effects, parallax for its own sake. Bauhaus restraint means the visual drama comes from scale, color block, and typographic contrast — not from motion.
- **Dense information design.** No data-dashboard density, no multi-column layouts trying to maximize above-fold content.

## Design Principles

1. **Structure over decoration.** Layout earns its place from content hierarchy, not visual filler. Grid is the rhythm section, not the soloist. Bauhaus principle: form follows function, and function is reading + browsing.

2. **Sharp contrast, sparingly.** The visual tension comes from deliberate clashes: thick vs. thin, heavy vs. light, full-bleed vs. generous margin. A committed four-hue palette (red, coral, burgundy, yellow) carries the surface, with warm-tinted neutrals as the resting state.

3. **Content-first typography.** Typography is the primary interface. Hierarchy through weight + scale contrast (≥1.25 ratio). Generous leading. Tight measure discipline (65–75ch). No decorative type moves.

4. **Breathing room is a feature.** Low information density. Whitespace is not "wasted space" — it's the reader's pause between thoughts. Cards only when they're the best affordance; never for filling space.

5. **Anti-generic at every scale.** From the color strategy to the hover transition to the way a code snippet is displayed — if someone could guess it from a framework's starter template, rewrite it. The site is a demonstration of taste, not a vehicle for content.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Adequate color contrast on all surfaces (the Bauhaus palette naturally supports this with its strong figure-ground separation). Reduced-motion support; no essential content or navigation depends on animation. Focus-visible outlines on all interactive elements. Semantic HTML baseline.
